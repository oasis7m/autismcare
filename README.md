# Background

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a developmental disorder that affects children's social communication, behavioral patterns and perception methods. According to data from the Global Health Organization, the number of children with autism is increasing year by year. The global average incidence rate is approximately 1% to 2%. Many of these children have missed the best time for early intervention due to delayed diagnosis, uneven distribution of intervention resources, or insufficient family awareness.

**In real life, children with autism and their families are confronted with multiple predicaments:**

ğŸ§© Long diagnostic cycle: Professional assessment requires queuing for months or even longer, resulting in delayed intervention

ğŸ§  Cognitive differences are hard to detect: mild autism or subclinical symptoms are often overlooked

ğŸ« imbalance of educational and rehabilitation resources: quality institutions are concentrated in big cities, and most families have difficulty obtaining continuous support

ğŸ‘¨â€ğŸ‘©â€ğŸ‘§ parents lack guidance: Many parents do not know how to interact with their children, assess their development or improve the effectiveness of training

Meanwhile, with the development of artificial intelligence (AI), computer vision and behavioral analysis models, we have the opportunity to make technology truly serve special groups, making the early screening, assessment and rehabilitation of children with autism earlier, faster, more accurate and more accessible.

In light of such realistic predicaments and technological opportunities, this project came into being.
